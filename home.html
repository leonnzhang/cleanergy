Welcome to Energy Production. Revolutionalizing a bunch of stuff Get Started
Inspiration We wanted to create an interactive and fun tool that allows people to see the difference between energy sources and experiment with changing various numbers to see the impact that will make on the final result. We also want to display the final result in a simple and visual way, so that an average user with no background knowledge could still see and understand the difference clearly.
What it does Once you press "get started" or scroll down, you will see our energy sources calculator. There, pick any energy source and enter your custom values to see how much that will cost, how much energy it generates, and the carbon emissions if applicable. We will also compare the cost, output and emissions to many real-world examples and provide an optimal energy source for your situation.

Challenges we ran into During the process of creating this, we ran into issues with finding reliable data that is easy to work within code. We found various APIs regarding weather and energy, but they all turned out to be paid, too limited or too complex and unfeasible to implement in our timeframe. Eventually, we settled by researching the calculations for different energy sources and simplifying them to only a few inputs and outputs so that any user with no expertise in the field could use and understand our project.

What's next for Energy Production Our goal for this project is to show average people with little knowledge on the subject how much of a difference clean energy can make. Hopefully, this will inspire them with enough interest to do further research on clean energy sources. By making people more aware of the problems regarding fossil fuels, there can be more people willing to make a difference by contributing to the development, distribution and implementation of clean energy.

Coal Coal is a solid fossil fuel formed by plant matter decomposing under the proper conditions, which can be burnt for electrical energy.
Oil Oil is a liquid fossil fuel formed by tiny organic matter decomposing under the proper conditions, which can be burnt for electrical energy.
Gas Natural gas is a solid fossil fuel formed by microscopic organic matter decomposing under the proper conditions, which can be burnt for electrical energy.
Solar Solar energy is created by using photovoltaic cells in solar panels to convert direct sunlight to electrical energy.
Wind Large turbines are installed in windy locations which spin and turn a generator which generates electrical energy.
Hydro Turbines are installed in areas with waterflow like dams or rivers that spin and turn a generator to create electrical energy.
